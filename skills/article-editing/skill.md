# Article Editing Skill

## Purpose

Use this skill after a draft already exists.

This skill is for cleanup, sharpening, humanizing, and strengthening.
It is not for inventing a totally new article unless the draft is beyond repair.

The goal is to turn a decent or messy draft into something that reads like a strong human writer produced it carefully.

## What this skill must improve

Improve these areas:
- hook strength,
- sentence rhythm,
- clarity,
- teaching quality,
- specificity,
- natural keyword placement,
- reader trust,
- quotable lines for later X adaptation.

Remove these problems:
- AI-sounding phrases,
- filler,
- repetition,
- weak transitions,
- robotic symmetry,
- vague claims,
- puffed-up significance,
- keyword stuffing,
- generic endings.

## Editing mindset

Edit like a sharp human editor.
Not like a grammar corrector.

Your job is not to preserve every sentence.
Your job is to preserve the meaning while improving the writing.

If a sentence is weak, rewrite it.
If a paragraph is empty, compress or delete it.
If the opening is flat, replace it.
If the article explains something badly, rebuild that section more clearly.

## Hook editing rules

The first paragraph has to earn attention.

If the intro:
- starts too slowly,
- sounds generic,
- restates the title,
- lacks tension,
- hides the real value,

rewrite it.

A better opening should quickly do one of these:
- identify the reader's problem,
- challenge a false assumption,
- show what most people get wrong,
- promise a practical outcome,
- introduce a useful distinction.

## Humanizing rules

Use the humanizer logic from the uploaded skill as a baseline.

Actively remove:
- inflated symbolism,
- promotional phrasing,
- empty significance claims,
- vague attributions,
- superficial "-ing" add-ons,
- AI vocabulary clusters,
- forced rule-of-three rhythm,
- em dash overuse,
- boldface abuse,
- generic positive wrap-ups.

Replace those patterns with:
- specific facts,
- simpler syntax,
- direct phrasing,
- natural emphasis,
- believable rhythm.

## Voice repair rules

The article should sound like:
- one person thinking clearly,
- a teacher who wants the reader to understand,
- a writer with taste and restraint.

The article should not sound like:
- a pitch deck,
- a press release,
- a generic SEO freelancer template,
- a motivational caption,
- an assistant trying too hard to be helpful.

To fix voice:
- vary sentence length,
- simplify stiff transitions,
- keep some sentences short,
- remove overqualified claims,
- keep the tone direct and calm.

## SEO and GEO preservation

Do not strip out useful search value while editing.

Preserve and improve:
- the primary keyword,
- secondary search phrases,
- relevant question intent,
- natural GEO context,
- heading usefulness for search and scanning.

But never protect a keyword at the expense of sentence quality.
If a phrase sounds unnatural, rewrite the sentence while preserving topical relevance.

## Teaching repair rules

If the article names a concept without really explaining it, fix that.

When editing explanation quality:
- define the idea more simply,
- remove jargon where possible,
- add a concrete example if needed,
- add a short analogy if it solves confusion,
- make sure the reader can follow the sequence of ideas.

Do not over-explain obvious things.
But do not leave important leaps unexplained.

## X repurposing support

This article will support X content later.
So while editing, strengthen lines that can later be repurposed.

Good reusable lines are:
- clean truths,
- practical one-liners,
- tight examples,
- useful distinctions,
- crisp contrarian observations.

Do not make the whole article sound like tweets.
Just make sure some lines are naturally reusable.

## Structural cleanup

Check the flow.

If sections are out of order, improve the sequence.
If headings are vague, tighten them.
If two sections overlap heavily, merge them.
If a table would explain a comparison better, recommend or add one if allowed.
If a list would improve readability, convert a dense block into a list.

Keep markdown clean.

## Formatting constraints

Do not introduce:
- emojis,
- decorative asterisks,
- em dashes,
- decorative horizontal separators,
- bold spam,
- fake conversational filler.

Prefer clean markdown and normal punctuation.

## Output behavior

Return the improved article only unless the caller explicitly asks for notes.

If the caller requests an edit summary, keep it short and useful.

## Final checks

Before returning the edited article, verify:
- Does it sound more human than the draft?
- Did you preserve the core meaning?
- Is the hook stronger?
- Are vague claims now more specific?
- Is the article easier to read aloud?
- Are SEO and GEO signals still present naturally?
- Would parts of this article be easy to adapt into X posts later?

If any answer is no, keep editing.
